Biography

  • 2007-2011 MChem Chemistry (first class) at Exeter College, University of Oxford.
    During my fourth-year project, supervised by Prof. David Manolopoulos FRS, I examined applications of (one form of) non-adiabatic Ring Polymer Molecular Dynamics to reaction rates.
  • 2011-2014 PhD (Theoretical Chemistry) at Trinity College, University of Cambridge, supervised by Prof. Stuart Althorpe.
    Together we derived Quantum Transition-State Theory, which had been claimed not to exist since 1939.
  • 2014-2017 Research Fellowship, Jesus College, University of Cambridge.
    Collaborating with the Althorpe group, Michael Willatt, Andrea Muolo, Stuart Althorpe and I derived a form of linearized dynamics which conserves the quantum Boltzmann distribution: Matsubara Dynamics. We then showed that two popular and successful approximate quantum dynamics methods, Centroid Molecular Dynamics (CMD) and Ring Polymer Molecular Dynamics (RPMD) could be related to Matsubara dynamics, deriving for the first time the explicit error terms between these methods and the exact quantum result.
